Hey, I'm Elliott Conrad
THRIFTLET
Co-Founder/iOS Engineer

At 20 years old, I Co-Founded my first company Thriftlet with my cousin Sam.

With no real knowledge of programming, let alone app development, I dove head first into Swift and XCode, discovering my passion for iOS development in the process. Unfortunately, my school didn't offer any courses on iOS development, so I took it upon myself to learn it in the evenings after class. Flash forward to now, I've learned so many skills that I wouldn't have had the chance to learn in school, all thanks to Thriftlet.

We were led to create Thriftlet due to the shortcomings we saw in current ecommerce applications. We sought to innovate on the idea of buying and selling items between friends and communities. Now, three years after writing the first piece of code, we are getting ready to launch on the app store by the end of the year and Sam and I couldn't be more excited.

Delta Air Lines

I'll never forget the state of disbelief I was in when my recruiter told me over the phone I had gotten the job. Landing an internship at such a prestigious company was a dream come true and I was overwhelmed with excitement and anxiety of what was to come.

I also learned what it takes to become a business professional--or at least get closer to it. By the end of the semester I was chosen to represent my department in front of other Co-ops and employees, showcasing not only my growth as an individual but also the projects I was able to implement during my time with the company.

While my time with Delta Air Lines was cut short due to the CVOID-19 pandemic, I will cherish my memories and knowledge I was able to obtain during my time there and I'll continue to keep climbing!

Georgia College & State University
B.S. in Computer Science - Summer 2021
Minor in Management Information Systems - Summer 2021

I'd be lying if I said I wasn't a little intimidated by the bigger and nationally renowned universities such as Georgia Tech and The University of Georgia.

However, I believe my education has been an advantage for me. My graduating class at Georgia College has roughly 25 students in it(for computer science), a mere fraction of the students graduating with a computer science degree from those other universities.

Yet, being one of two dozen students in my classes allows me a level of personal attention from professors that is unparallel to bigger schools. My professors know me not as just a student, but as Elliott, which its importance cannot be overstated. Perhaps the most important thing Georgia College has shown me so far, is my passion for programming which has led me to Co-Found Thriftlet and program many other projects.
